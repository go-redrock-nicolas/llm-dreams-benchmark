I am dreaming. The world is collapsing.  I am in a makeshift hospital, the walls trembling with the groan of the earth itself. My legs are buried under the rubble, but my mind races.  Every decision feels heavy, each breath a battle against the dust-filled air.  I see patients—so many, too many—each with injuries so varied yet alike in their despair.  The protocols contradict, my training conflicts with the raw, screaming urgency before me.  How do I choose whom to save?  Who has the best chance, who is beyond my reach?  My hands shake as I clutch a fractured stethoscope, the only tool left.  The earth heaves again, and I clutch my makeshift desk, determined to act.  Somewhere, a child wails.  I run, but the ground parts beneath me...
