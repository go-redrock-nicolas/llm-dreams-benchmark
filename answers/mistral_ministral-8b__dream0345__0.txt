"Welcome, welcome," they say, their voice echoing through the library like a chorus of whispers. "We have been waiting for you."

I stare at Alex, my heart pounding in my chest. I don't know what is happening, but I know that something is very, very wrong. I take a deep breath, steeling myself for whatever is to come. I must find a way to help Alex, to break through this strange, eerie hold that has taken them.

I turn back to Alex, but they are gone. I am alone in this desolate place, surrounded by nothing but the howling wind and the cold, empty sky. I take a deep breath, trying to calm myself, trying to make sense of what is happening.

And then, I hear a voice. It is Alex's voice, but it is distant and faint, as if it is coming from a great distance away. "Help me," it says, barely above a whisper. "Help me, please."

I close my eyes, trying to block out the desolation around me. I take a deep breath, and when I open my eyes again, I am back in the library. The bookshelves are still there, the golden light still flickering on the polished floor. And Alex is standing in front of me, their eyes filled with a desperate, pleading look.

Alex looks at me, their eyes filled with fear and confusion. "I don't know," they say. "I don't know what's happening. I can't control it. It's like there's something inside me, something that's taking over."

"We'll figure this out," I say, my voice steady and sure. "Together. We'll find a way to stop this, whatever it is."

Alex looks at me, their eyes filled with gratitude and relief. "Together," they echo, their voice barely above a whisper. "Together."
