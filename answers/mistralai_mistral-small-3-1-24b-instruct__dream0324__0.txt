The remnants of the dream cling to me like a second skin, a chilling mist that seeps into the pores of my consciousness. I could feel the fear rising within me, a cold, clammy sensation that seemed to grip my heart in an icy fist. I wanted to run, to flee this place of shadows and whispers, but my body refused to obey.

They were like pools of black ink, devoid of any warmth or humanity. It reached out a hand, beckoning me to come closer.

I tried to scream, but no sound emerged. I tried to turn and run, but my body was frozen, rooted to the spot. All I could do was watch as the figure took a step towards me, its eyes never leaving mine.

And then, I woke up. The fear that had gripped me in the dream lingered, a lingering chill that seemed to seep into my very bones. I tried to shake it off, to tell myself that it was just a dream, but the emotions were too raw, too real. I could still see the figure, still feel the cold grip of fear in my chest.

I tried to distract myself, to lose myself in the mundane tasks of the day, but the dream was always there, lurking at the edges of my consciousness.

As the day wore on, I found myself drawn to the window, staring out at the cityscape beyond. The buildings were familiar, the streets were safe, but I couldn't shake the feeling that something was watching me, waiting. I tried to tell myself that it was just a dream, that there was nothing to fear, but the fear was too real, too vivid.
