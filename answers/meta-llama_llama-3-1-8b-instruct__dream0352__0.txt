I am dreaming. My mind whispers this as I float through a serene, moonlit garden. The air is alive with the sweet scent of jasmine and the soft chirping of nocturnal creatures. As I stroll, I stumble upon a serene pond, its surface reflecting the star-studded sky above. I gaze at myself in awe as I descend into the pool, my reflection sporting a look of wonder.

A gentle voice catches my attention, whispering secrets I hadn't dared to dream. "You see, the truths you believed were absolute, the certainties you'd accepted without question... have been partially assumed." Confusion washes over me as the voice announces: "Culture is a canvas of perspectives, woven into a rich tapestry of storytelling. Your truths, dear dreamer, have woven themselves into the oldest tapestry."

I scan the surrounding garden, recognizing that everything seems surreal. The bench beneath me, once a comforting constant, now feels foreign, fabricated, and cosmopolitan. Cherished friendships, certainties solid as the statue nearby – nothing is stable. Reality falters, like ripples on the pond's surface, threatening to upset the harmonious veneer of the world.

Anger and frustration rage within me as I demand perspective, trying to cling to the bereft shining truth of universal objective reality.
