I am dreaming. The weight of the earth presses down upon my shoulders as I venture deeper into the labyrinthine heart of the cave. The air grows colder, heavier, and the silence is a tangible force, pressing against my eardrums like a thick, damp cloth. The cave walls are slick with moisture, glistening in the faint glow of my flickering lantern. The light dances and flickers, casting eerie shadows that twist and writhe like spectral serpents. The air is filled with the scent of damp earth and the faint, metallic tang of minerals, a scent that clings to the back of my throat and leaves a bitter taste on my tongue.

Suddenly, the ground beneath my feet begins to tremble. The vibrations start as a low rumble, like distant thunder, but quickly grow in intensity. The walls of the cave groan and creak, and I can see the cracks widening, the stones shifting and grinding against one another. Panic surges through me as the earthquake intensifies, and I stumble, my lantern flying from my grasp. It crashes against the cave floor, the glass shattering and the flame extinguishing in a puff of smoke.

Now, the cave is plunged into darkness. Absolute, impenetrable darkness. I can't see my hand in front of my face, and the sound of my own breathing seems unnaturally loud, echoing through the void. Fear grips me like a vice, squeezing the air from my lungs. I can feel the cold seeping into my bones, the darkness pressing in on me from all sides, suffocating me.

But then, something else. A faint, distant sound. A soft, rhythmic tapping. Like... like footsteps. I strain my ears, listening intently. Yes, there it is again. Tap-tap-tap. It's coming from deeper within the cave. I hesitate, my heart pounding in my chest. Should I follow it? Or should I stay here, in the darkness, waiting for the earthquake to subside?

My breath comes in ragged gasps as I make my decision. I take a deep breath, steeling myself against the fear, and begin to follow the sound. Tap-tap-tap. It echoes through the darkness, guiding me deeper into the heart of the cave. The darkness is absolute, but I press on, driven by a primal need to survive, to find some semblance of light or safety. Tap-tap-tap. The sound grows louder, closer. I can feel the cold seeping into my bones, but I don't stop. I can't stop. Not until I know what lies ahead.
